DOPANT ATOMS AND ENERGY LEVELS
The real power of semiconductors is realized by adding small, controlled amounts of 
specific dopant or impurity atoms. 
This doping process, can greatly alter the electrical characteristics of the 
semiconductor. The doped semiconductor, called an extrinsic material.
22

вһў
With a small amount of energy, such as thermal 
energy the donor electron, can be elevated into 
the conduction band, leaving behind a positively 
charged ion.
вһў
The electron in the conduction band can now 
move through the crystal generating a current, 
while the positively charged ion is fixed.
вһў
This type of impurity atom donates an electron 
to the conduction band and so is called a donor 
impurity atoms (Group 5 elements).
вһў
The donor impurity atoms add electrons to the 
conduction band without creating holes in the 
valence band. The resulting material is referred 
to as a (n-type for the negatively charged

вһў
As the temperature increases, ni increases, and EF moves closer to the intrinsic 
Fermi level.
вһў
At high temperature, the semiconductor material begins to lose its extrinsic 
characteristics and begins to behave more like an intrinsic semiconductor. 
вһў
At absolute zero degrees, all energy states below EF are full and all energy states 
above EF are empty.

DEGENERATE AND NON-DEGENERATE 
SEMICONDUCTORS
вһў
Till now we have implicitly assumed that the concentration of dopant atoms added 
is small when compared to the density of host or semiconductor atoms.
вһў
The small number of impurity atoms are spread far enough apart so that there is no 
interaction between dopant energy states.
вһў
These types of semiconductors are referred to as non-degenerate semiconductors.
54
Monday, February 3, 2025
POSD (Dr. Bhaskar Awadhiya)
вһў
If the impurity concentration increases, the distance between the impurity atoms
decreases and a point will be reached when donor or acceptor states will begin
to interact with each other. 
вһў
As the donor concentration further increases, the band of donor states widens and 
may overlap the bottom of the conduction band. This overlap occurs when the 
donor concentration becomes comparable with the effective density of states.
